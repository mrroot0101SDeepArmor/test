🍕 Pizza · classic & contemporary

Pizza is one of the world’s most beloved foods. Originating in Naples, Italy, it has evolved into countless regional styles. At its core, pizza is a baked flatbread topped with tomato sauce, cheese, and various ingredients.

Neapolitan pizza is the traditional style: a thin, soft crust with San Marzano tomatoes, fresh mozzarella, basil, and olive oil. It is cooked in a wood-fired oven at very high heat for just 60–90 seconds.

New York–style pizza features a large, hand-tossed crust that is crisp and chewy. It is typically sold in wide slices and topped with a rich tomato sauce and mozzarella. Common toppings include pepperoni, sausage, mushrooms, and olives.

Chicago deep-dish is a hearty variation: a buttery, high-edged crust filled with cheese, chunky tomato sauce, and layers of meat or vegetables. It is more like a savoury pie than a flatbread.

Beyond these classics, pizza continues to inspire creativity – from gluten-free bases to vegan cheeses and unexpected toppings like figs, prosciutto, or even honey. It remains a universal comfort food.

🍣 Sushi · art of simplicity

Sushi is a Japanese culinary tradition centred on vinegared rice, known as shari, combined with seafood, vegetables, and occasionally tropical fruits. It is not just food; it is an art form that balances flavour, texture, and presentation.

Nigiri is the most iconic form: a hand-pressed mound of rice topped with a slice of fresh fish or seafood, often lightly brushed with soy sauce or wasabi. Common nigiri toppings include salmon, tuna, shrimp, and eel.

Maki (rolls) are made by wrapping rice and fillings in a sheet of nori (seaweed). Variants like the California roll (crab, avocado, cucumber) and the spicy tuna roll have become global favourites. Uramaki is an inside-out roll with rice on the outside.

Sashimi is simply fresh, raw fish sliced thinly and served without rice – it highlights the pure taste of the ingredient. Popular sashimi choices are yellowtail, mackerel, and scallop.

High-quality sushi relies on the freshest ingredients and the skill of the itamae (chef). The combination of vinegared rice, umami-rich fish, and subtle garnishes like ginger and wasabi creates a balanced and refined dining experience.

🥗 Grain bowl · wholesome & vibrant

Buddha bowls (or grain bowls) are a modern, nutrient-packed meal built around a base of whole grains, fresh vegetables, plant-based protein, and a flavourful dressing. They are endlessly customisable and visually colourful.

The base is often quinoa, brown rice, farro, or barley. These grains provide a hearty texture and a good source of fibre and minerals. Some bowls use mixed greens or roasted sweet potatoes as an alternative base.

Vegetables play a starring role: roasted broccoli, bell peppers, shredded carrots, avocado, radishes, and leafy greens like kale or spinach. They add crunch, sweetness, and a wide range of vitamins.

Protein can come from grilled tofu, tempeh, chickpeas, lentils, or shredded chicken. The bowl is then finished with a drizzle of tahini, lemon-herb vinaigrette, or a spicy peanut sauce. Seeds and nuts (sesame, pumpkin, almonds) add extra texture.

This style of eating is appreciated for its balance of macronutrients, vibrant colours, and the freedom to use seasonal produce. It is a wholesome, satisfying meal that works for lunch or dinner.

🍲 Stew · slow & soulful

Stew is a dish of solid ingredients (meat, fish, or vegetables) cooked slowly in a liquid, often served with its own juices as a sauce. It is a timeless cooking method found in every culture, prized for its depth of flavour and tenderness.

Beef bourguignon is a French classic: beef braised in red wine with onions, carrots, and mushrooms, flavoured with garlic and herbs. The long cooking time transforms tough cuts into meltingly tender morsels.

Irish stew traditionally uses lamb or mutton with potatoes, onions, and parsley. It is a simple, hearty dish that reflects the rustic cuisine of Ireland. In the US, beef stew often includes root vegetables like carrots, celery, and parsnips in a rich beef broth.

Vegetarian stews are equally satisfying: lentil and vegetable stew, chickpea and spinach tagine, or a rustic mushroom and barley pot. They rely on aromatic spices, tomatoes, and slow simmering to develop a savoury, complex taste.

Stew is more than a meal – it is a comforting ritual, often better the next day, and perfect for sharing with family and friends on cool evenings.
